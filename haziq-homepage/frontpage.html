    The April 2000 flood demonstrated how ill-prepared Edinburgh is for extreme weather events brought on by Climate Change. Due to the failure of traditional structures, there has been a move towards natural defences. Natural river and flood management encourage a natural flow of the river and storage of water in plains, while also increasing water infiltration, but these strategies require a large amount of space.
     
    Steps have been taken to introduce defences at Scottish Environmental Protection Agency (SEPA) suggested opportunity sites, but many of these have missed the opportunity to create valuable green spaces for deprived communities. By putting more weight on deprivation, we set out to find a new location of opportunity along the Water of Leith, which covers the areas under the most flood threat in Edinburgh. Through this, we have identified     Murray Burn as a potential site for river management implementation.
